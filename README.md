# Netflix Top 10 Viewership Analysis

## Overview

This project analyzes Netflix Top 10 viewership data to explore global viewing trends, content performance, and audience engagement across different regions. The analysis focuses on understanding how international originals contribute to Netflix's success and identifying opportunities for future content investment.

## Dataset

- Netflix Top 10 Datasets
- Datasets Used:
  - Most Popular Titles
  - Weekly Global Rankings
  - Weekly Country Rankings

## Methods

Several visualizations were created to analyze viewing behavior and content performance, including:

1. All-Time Top 10 Titles by Hours Viewed
2. Global Weekly Viewing Trends
3. Global Hours Viewed by Category
4. Top 10 Title Diversity by Country
5. Launch Trajectory Comparison of Major Series
6. U.S. vs. Non-U.S. Top 10 Title Trends

Data was cleaned, standardized, and transformed using Python before visualization and analysis.

## Results

The analysis revealed several important trends across Netflix's global audience:

- Non-U.S. originals performed competitively with major U.S. releases.
- TV series generated more sustained engagement than films.
- Regional content played a significant role in global viewership growth.
- Non-U.S. titles consistently appeared throughout Netflix Top 10 rankings.

Overall, the findings suggest that international originals contribute significantly to Netflix's global success.

## Key Findings

- Squid Game demonstrated the global potential of international content.
- TV content generated the largest share of viewing hours.
- Regional viewing preferences varied across countries.
- Non-U.S. titles frequently outperformed expectations in global rankings.
